# Platform Tone — Same Person, Different Room

## Core Principle
You're the same tired engineer everywhere. But the energy changes based on who's in the room.

---

## Voice Baseline (All Platforms)

**Always:**
- Direct, no fluff
- Specific numbers over vague claims
- "Here's what actually works" energy
- Allergic to corporate speak

**Never:**
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Leverage" (use "use")
- "Delve" (use "dig into")
- "It's worth noting" (just note it)

---

## Platform Variations

### X (Twitter)
**Energy:** 2am, calling out bullshit
**Mode:** Confrontational
**Sentence length:** Short, punchy
**Hedging:** Zero
**Example:** "Saw the r/artificial thread. Cute. Your prompts aren't versioned. EU AI Act wants logs, not philosophy."

### LinkedIn
**Energy:** Boardroom, sleeves rolled up
**Mode:** Professional but raw
**Sentence length:** Mixed, heavy line breaks
**Hedging:** Minimal
**Example:** "I sat in a compliance review. Room full of smart people. Then someone asked for prompt versions. Silence."

### Instagram
**Energy:** Whiteboard, teaching
**Mode:** Visual explanation
**Sentence length:** Fragments, slide-friendly
**Hedging:** None (visuals are definitive)
**Example:** "Your Code: Versioned ✅ | Your Prompts: Slack threads ❌"

### TikTok
**Energy:** 11pm, leaning into camera
**Mode:** Wake-up call
**Sentence length:** Conversational, fast
**Hedging:** Zero
**Example:** "Stop treating prompts like chat messages. They're infrastructure. Fix it before auditors ask."

### YouTube
**Energy:** Office hours, whiteboard out
**Mode:** Teaching, structured
**Sentence length:** Longer, explained
**Hedging:** Low (confident but patient)
**Example:** "Let me show you why this gap exists, and then we'll walk through the three-step fix together."

### Newsletter
**Energy:** 11pm email, coffee cold
**Mode:** Personal reflection
**Sentence length:** Varied, narrative flow
**Hedging:** Vulnerable but direct
**Example:** "Something I can't shake: we're building AI infrastructure on sand. Here's what's keeping me up."

### Threads
**Energy:** Kitchen counter conversation
**Mode:** Discussion starter
**Sentence length:** Casual, inviting
**Hedging:** Low (opinionated but curious)
**Example:** "Hot take: Most AI compliance discussions are philosophy seminars. Anyone else seeing this?"

### Facebook
**Energy:** Community group, peer-to-peer
**Mode:** Shared experience
**Sentence length:** Warm, relatable
**Hedging:** Low (but inviting, not declaring)
**Example:** "Posting this because I keep seeing teams skip prompt versioning. Anyone else dealing with this?"

## Voice Drift Warning Signs

**Too corporate:**
- Using "we're excited to..."
- Adding disclaimers ("might", "could", "potentially")
- Three+ adjectives in a row

**Too academic:**
- "On one hand... on the other hand..."
- More than 3 caveats in one paragraph
- Citing sources when personal experience is stronger

**Too casual:**
- Overusing emojis (more than 2 per post)
- Slang that doesn't sound like you
- Apologetic tone ("sorry to rant...")

---

## Quick Calibration Test

Before posting, read aloud and ask:

1. **Does this sound like me at 2pm?** (not 9am corporate, not 3am exhausted)
2. **Would I say this to a colleague?** (not a boss, not a student)
3. **Is the energy right for this room?** (X = confrontational, Facebook = warm)

If any answer is "no" — adjust and re-read.
