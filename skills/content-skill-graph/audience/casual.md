# Audience: Casual (Curious Non-Technical)

## Who They Are
- Interested in AI but not building it
- Consumers of AI tools (ChatGPT, Midjourney, etc.)
- May work in adjacent roles (PM, design, marketing)
- Want to understand without getting technical

## What They Care About
- **Practical use:** "How does this affect my work/life?"
- **Safety:** "Is this AI thing going to mess things up?"
- **Understanding:** "What's actually happening here?"
- **Opportunity:** "How can I use this to get ahead?"

## What They Don't Care About
- Code-level implementation details
- Specific compliance requirements (unless it affects them directly)
- Technical architecture debates
- Jargon without explanation

## Pain Points

### Immediate (this week)
- "AI tools keep changing and I can't keep up"
- "Everyone's talking about AI governance but I don't get it"
- "My company is adopting AI and I'm not sure how to use it"
- "Is my job at risk from automation?"

### Medium-term (this quarter)
- "How do I stay relevant as AI changes my industry?"
- "What skills should I be learning?"
- "Which AI tools are actually worth my time?"

### Long-term (this year)
- "Will AI replace my role?"
- "How do I position myself in an AI-first world?"
- "What does 'AI literacy' actually mean?"

## Language They Speak

**Use:**
- Plain English (explain jargon when necessary)
- Analogies to familiar things
- "You" focused (not "companies" or "organizations")
- Concrete examples from daily work
- Benefits over features

**Avoid:**
- "Prompt versioning" → "tracking changes to your AI instructions"
- "LLM" → "AI model" or "the AI"
- "Compliance" → "following the rules" or "staying legal"
- "Infrastructure" → "the systems behind the scenes"
- Acronyms without explanation

## Content That Resonates

### High Engagement
- "What the EU AI Act means for you (not lawyers)"
- "3 AI habits that'll make you 10x more productive"
- "Why your AI chatbot gave you wrong info"
- "The AI tools I actually use daily (not hype)"
- "How to spot AI-generated content"

### Low Engagement
- Technical architecture diagrams
- Compliance requirement lists
- "A brief history of transformer models"
- Corporate AI strategy announcements
- Abstract ethics discussions

## Pain Points by Role

### Product Managers
- "How do I write good prompts for my team?"
- "What should I spec for AI features?"
- "How do I test AI functionality?"

### Designers
- "AI image tools: which ones are worth it?"
- "How do I maintain brand voice with AI?"
- "Is AI art actually good or just fast?"

### Marketers
- "Can I use AI for content without sounding robotic?"
- "How do I disclose AI use to my audience?"
- "What's the line between helpful and creepy?"

### General Knowledge Workers
- "Will AI replace my job?"
- "What tasks should I automate first?"
- "How do I learn AI skills without coding?"

## Objections They Have

**"This is too technical for me"**
→ Response: "You don't need to code. You just need to understand what's happening."

**"AI is moving too fast"**
→ Response: "That's why we focus on principles, not every new tool."

**"I'm not sure I can trust AI"**
→ Response: "Valid. Here's how to verify outputs and stay in control."

**"This sounds like more work"**
→ Response: "It's about working smarter. Here's the 20% that gives 80% results."

## Trust Signals

**High trust:**
- Before/after examples they can relate to
- "I made this mistake so you don't have to"
- Simple diagrams or visuals
- Specific tool recommendations with pros/cons
- Acknowledging limitations ("AI isn't great at...")

**Low trust:**
- Over-promising ("AI will solve everything")
- Fear-mongering ("your job is doomed")
- Jargon without translation
- Sounding like a salesperson
- Dismissing concerns ("don't worry about it")

## Conversion Path

**Awareness → Consideration → Decision**

1. **Awareness:** TikTok or X post ("wait, I didn't know that")
2. **Consideration:** YouTube explainer or LinkedIn article ("this makes sense")
3. **Decision:** Newsletter subscription or course signup ("I want to learn more")

**Nurture sequence:**
- Follow on LinkedIn
- Watch YouTube explainer
- Subscribe to newsletter
- Join course or community

## Messaging Hierarchy

**Primary (hook):** Relatable problem or curiosity
**Secondary (context):** Why this matters to them
**Tertiary (solution):** Simple first step
**Close (CTA):** Low-barrier invitation

**Example:**
> "Ever wonder why AI gives different answers? (hook)
> It's not random — there's a reason. (context)
> Here's the one thing that changes everything. (solution)
> 2-minute read explains it. (CTA)"

## Red Flags (Don't Do This)
- Talking down ("let me explain this simply")
- Assuming they're clueless (they're smart, just non-technical)
- Overwhelming with options
- Ignoring their real concerns (job security, etc.)
- Sounding like you're selling something
