# Hooks — 6 Formulas That Stop the Scroll

## Core Principle
The hook isn't the first sentence. It's the first **feeling**.

---

## Formula 1: The Callout
**Pattern:** "Most [role] get [thing] wrong. Here's why."

**Why it works:** Challenges identity, creates curiosity gap

**Examples:**
- "Most CTOs get AI governance wrong. They're building policy when they need engineering."
- "You're optimizing model costs. But your prompt waste is 10x higher."
- "Everyone's building AI agents. Nobody's asking if they should."

**Use when:** Common practice is actually wrong

**Avoid when:** You're just contrarian for attention

---

## Formula 2: The Gap
**Pattern:** "You [good thing]. But you [bad thing]. Fix it."

**Why it works:** Validates then exposes, creates tension

**Examples:**
- "You version your code. But your prompts live in Slack. That's a compliance gap."
- "You track model drift. But not prompt changes. Same problem, different layer."
- "You have CI/CD. But prompts go straight to prod. Without tests."

**Use when:** There's a clear inconsistency in behavior

**Avoid when:** Both things are actually fine

---

## Formula 3: The Silence
**Pattern:** "We could show [good thing]. Then someone asked [hard question]. Silence."

**Why it works:** Story tension, relatable moment

**Examples:**
- "We could show every code change. Then someone asked for the prompt version. Silence."
- "The demo was perfect. Then the compliance officer asked about audit trails. Silence."
- "Model accuracy was great. Then they asked about data lineage. Silence."

**Use when:** You have a specific moment of realization

**Avoid when:** It's hypothetical (use The Gap instead)

---

## Formula 4: The Number Drop
**Pattern:** "[Specific stat]. That's the problem."

**Why it works:** Concrete, authoritative, skimmable

**Examples:**
- "3 camps arguing about AI ownership. All missing the point."
- "6 hours debugging model drift. Turned out to be an unlogged prompt change."
- "429 days until EU AI Act enforcement. Your prompts still aren't versioned."

**Use when:** You have a specific number that matters

**Avoid when:** The number is vague or made up

---

## Formula 5: The Reality Check
**Pattern:** "[Popular opinion] is noise. [Unpopular truth] is signal."

**Why it works:** Cuts through hype, positions you as clear-eyed

**Examples:**
- "The AI ownership debate is noise. Prompt traceability is signal."
- "Model benchmarks are noise. Production reliability is signal."
- "Agent frameworks are noise. Prompt hygiene is signal."

**Use when:** Everyone's focused on the wrong thing

**Avoid when:** The "popular opinion" is actually correct

---

## Formula 6: The Confession
**Pattern:** "I was wrong about [thing]. Here's what changed."

**Why it works:** Vulnerability builds trust, shows growth

**Examples:**
- "I was wrong about AI governance. Thought it was policy. It's engineering."
- "I spent 3 years optimizing models. Turns out prompts were the bottleneck."
- "I argued against compliance. Now I'm building the audit trails."

**Use when:** You genuinely changed your mind

**Avoid when:** It's a humble-brag ("I was too humble...")

---

## Hook Selection Matrix

| Topic Type | Best Formula |
|------------|--------------|
| Common mistake | The Callout |
| Inconsistency | The Gap |
| Personal realization | The Silence or Confession |
| Data-driven insight | The Number Drop |
| Hype vs reality | The Reality Check |
| Changed perspective | The Confession |

## Forbidden Hook Patterns

**Don't use:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..." (corporate filler)
- "I'm excited to share..." (announcement, not hook)
- "This will change everything" (hype, not substance)
- "You won't believe..." (clickbait, erodes trust)
- Questions that are actually statements ("Did you know most teams...?")

**Do use:**
- Specific numbers
- Personal moments
- Clear contradictions
- Direct challenges
- Genuine curiosity

## Hook Length by Platform

| Platform | Ideal Hook Length |
|----------|-------------------|
| X | 10-15 words (first 10 words must hook) |
| LinkedIn | 15-25 words (first 2 lines before "...see more") |
| Instagram | 5-10 words (slide 1, large text) |
| TikTok | 3-7 words (first 2 seconds, text overlay) |
| YouTube | 5-10 words (title + thumbnail text) |
| Newsletter | 5-10 words (subject line, mobile preview) |
| Threads | 10-20 words (first post) |
| Facebook | 15-25 words (first line before truncation) |
