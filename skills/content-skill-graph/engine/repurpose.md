# Repurposing Chain

**Version:** 1.0  
**Purpose:** Turn one blog post or topic into 8 platform-native posts that each THINK differently

---

## The Core Principle

**Rethink, don't reformat.**

Most content systems take one piece and shrink/expand it for each platform. That's reformatting. It produces 8 copies of the same idea, and anyone following you on multiple platforms will tune out.

This system produces 8 pieces that each **think about the topic differently** — different angle, different hook, different structure, different depth.

**Litmus test:** If someone followed you on ALL platforms, would they be annoyed seeing the same thing everywhere?

- **Yes** → You're reformatting. Go back.
- **No** → You've rethought it. Ship it.

---

## The Chain Order

Write in this order. Each step builds on the previous but adapts.

### Step 1: [[x]] (Write First)

**Why first:** X forces brevity. Starting here makes you find the core idea and the sharpest hook. Everything else expands from this.

**Output:** One tweet (280 chars) or thread (5-10 tweets)

**Angle:** Contrarian, hook-driven, punchy

**Example:**
> "You don't need a content team. You need 30 markdown files. Here's how I run 10 accounts without writing a single post manually:"

---

### Step 2: [[linkedin]] (Expand with Narrative)

**What changes:** Add personal story, professional framing, deeper analysis. Don't just add words — add a DIFFERENT ANGLE.

**Output:** Long-form post (1,300-2,000 chars)

**Angle:** Personal narrative with business lesson

**Example:**
> X says: "You don't need a content team. You need 30 markdown files."
>
> LinkedIn says: "6 months ago I was spending $8,000/month on content production across 10 platforms. Today I spend $0. Here's exactly what changed."

---

### Step 3: [[instagram]] (Make it Visual)

**What changes:** Extract key points, turn into carousel slides. Angle shifts to visual-first, scannable, aspirational.

**Output:** 7-10 slide carousel outline + caption

**Angle:** Bold claim on slide 1, one idea per slide

**Example:**
> Slide 1: "I Run 10 Accounts And Don't Write Anything"
> Slide 2-7: The system broken into visual steps
> Slide 8: "Save this. Follow for more"

---

### Step 4: [[tiktok]] (Make it Raw)

**What changes:** Condense to 45-60 second script. Angle is SHOWING not telling.

**Output:** Video script with timestamps (hook, context, how, result, CTA)

**Angle:** Raw screen recording, "let me show you" energy

**Example:**
> [0:00-0:02] "You're still writing content manually for every platform?"
> [0:02-0:10] "Let me show you what I use instead."
> [0:10-0:50] [Screen recording of folder structure + Claude generating posts]
> [0:50-0:55] "Link in bio for the full system."

---

### Step 5: [[youtube]] (Make it Deep)

**What changes:** Combine everything into structured tutorial. Viewer should be able to REPLICATE.

**Output:** Full video outline (8-12 min) with SEO title, description, timestamps

**Angle:** Educational, step-by-step, evergreen

**Example:**
> Title: "How to Run 10 Social Media Accounts with AI (Complete System Walkthrough)"
>
> [0:00-0:30] Hook: "I run 10 accounts and don't write manually"
> [0:30-2:00] Context: Why this matters, what most get wrong
> [2:00-9:00] Main: Step-by-step walkthrough with demos
> [9:00-10:00] Recap + CTA

---

### Step 6: [[newsletter]] (Make it Personal)

**What changes:** Deepest, most personal take. Behind-the-scenes context, lessons, exclusive insights not shared on social.

**Output:** 1,500-2,000 word email

**Angle:** Like a letter from a friend who's also your mentor

**Example:**
> "This week I want to pull back the curtain on something I've been building quietly for months. It's replaced $8k/mo in content costs and honestly changed how I think about AI collaboration..."

---

### Step 7: [[threads]] (Make it Conversational)

**What changes:** Adapt X version with more relaxed, opinion-driven angle.

**Output:** 1-3 short posts (under 500 chars each)

**Angle:** Shower thought, hot take, "thinking out loud"

**Example:**
> "hot take: the future of content isn't AI that writes for you. it's AI that thinks like 10 different people for you"

---

### Step 8: [[facebook]] (Make it Community)

**What changes:** Add discussion question. Frame as invitation to share, not just consume.

**Output:** Post with question at end (300-600 chars)

**Angle:** Community member, not thought leader

**Example:**
> "Has anyone else tried building a system to manage multiple social accounts at once? Here's what I've been experimenting with — curious what you all think."

## What Actually Changes Between Platforms

| Element | How It Adapts |
|---------|---------------|
| **Angle** | Different entry point into the same topic |
| **Hook** | Rewritten per platform (see [[hooks]]) |
| **Tone** | Adjusted per [[platform-tone]] |
| **Format** | Thread vs carousel vs video script vs long-form |
| **Length** | 280 chars → 2,000 words |
| **Depth** | Surface insight → deep tutorial |
| **CTA** | Platform-native (reply, save, subscribe, click) |

## The Rethinking Checklist

Before publishing any platform output, ask:

1. **Angle:** Does this approach the topic from a different angle than the other platforms?
2. **Hook:** Is the opener platform-native (not just copied from X)?
3. **Structure:** Does the format match how people consume on this platform?
4. **Depth:** Am I giving this platform the right level of detail?
5. **Voice:** Does this sound like us but adapted for this room?
6. **Value:** Could someone follow us on ALL platforms and not feel like they're seeing the same thing?

If any answer is "no" → rethink that platform's output.

## Batch Workflow

**Weekly batch session (Sunday or Monday, 60-90 min):**

1. Choose 2-3 topics for the week (from blog posts, trending news, original insights)
2. Run the full repurpose chain for each topic
3. This gives you 16-24 posts for the whole week in one sitting
4. Schedule everything using [[scheduling]]
5. Done. Go live your life.

**Daily (15 min):**
- Check engagement
- Reply to comments in first hour (algo hack)
- Note what hooks/formats performed

**Weekly review (Friday, 30 min):**
- What topics performed best? → More of those
- What hook types drove engagement? → Update [[hooks]]
- Which platform growing fastest? → Double down
- Any new content ideas from comments/DMs? → Add to backlog

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | What It Looks Like | Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| Reformatting | Same text, different lengths | Rethink the angle for each platform |
| Hook copying | Same opener everywhere | Use [[hooks]] to select platform-specific hooks |
| Voice drift | Sounds corporate on LinkedIn, too casual on YouTube | Re-read [[platform-tone]] before each platform |
| Wrong depth | 200-word YouTube description, 2,000-word X thread | Check [[scheduling]] for length guidelines |
| Skipping the chain | Writing LinkedIn first, then X | Always write X first — it forces clarity |

## Example: One Topic, 8 Angles

**Topic:** "AI cost governance for EU startups"

| Platform | Angle | Hook |
|----------|-------|------|
| X | Contrarian | "Your AI stack isn't just your problem anymore. The EU AI Act is turning governance debates into compliance requirements." |
| LinkedIn | Personal narrative | "6 months ago I thought prompt management was optional. Then a regulator asked me to reproduce a decision chain..." |
| Instagram | Visual proof | 7-slide carousel: "The 3 camps in the AI governance debate (and why almost no one argues for status quo)" |
| TikTok | Show don't tell | 45-sec: "POV: A regulator asks why your AI made this hiring decision and you say 'the LLM decided'" |
| YouTube | Tutorial | "How to Build Decision Traceability for EU AI Act Compliance" (8-min walkthrough) |
| Newsletter | Behind-the-scenes | "This week I'm pulling back the curtain on the governance debate that's coming for your stack..." |
| Threads | Hot take | "hot take: AI governance isn't about controlling the models. it's about controlling your prompts" |
| Facebook | Discussion | "Anyone else navigating EU AI Act compliance? What's your approach to prompt traceability?" |

Same topic. Eight completely different pieces. Each valuable even to someone who follows you everywhere.
