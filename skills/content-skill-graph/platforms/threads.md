# Threads — Conversational, Opinion-Driven

## Role
The watercooler. The "what do you think?" that sparks discussion.

## Audience
- Early adopters, tech-forward
- People who read and reply
- Community builders

## DNA
- **Angle:** Conversational opinion or question
- **Hook:** "Hot take:" or "Quick question:"
- **Tone:** Casual, inviting, slightly provocative
- **Format:** 1-5 connected posts, 300-500 chars each
- **Depth:** One point + invite discussion

## Rules

### Must Have
- Hook in first post (clear stance or question)
- Invite replies (end with question or "thoughts?")
- Reply to early comments (first 2 hours)
- Keep it conversational (not a lecture)

### Never
- Corporate voice ("We're excited to share...")
- More than 5 connected posts (use LinkedIn for long-form)
- Argue with every reply (pick your battles)
- Delete unpopular takes (own your words)

## Hook Formulas

### 1. The Hot Take
> "Hot take: [contrarian opinion]. Change my mind."

### 2. The Question
> "Quick question for [role]: How do you handle [thing]?"

### 3. The Observation
> "Noticing a pattern: [observation]. Anyone else seeing this?"

### 4. The Confession
> "Admitting something: I was wrong about [thing]."

## Format Specs

- **Length:** 300-500 chars per post
- **Chain:** 1-5 connected posts max
- **Tone:** Casual, like talking to a colleague
- **Emojis:** Use naturally (not forced)

## Structure

```
Post 1: Hook (opinion or question)
Post 2: Context (why this matters)
Post 3: Example (personal experience)
Post 4: Takeaway (what I learned)
Post 5: CTA (what do you think?)
```

## Examples

### Bad (Reformat)
> "Just published a new blog post on AI governance! Check it out: [link]"

### Good (Rethink)
**Post 1:** "Hot take: Most AI compliance discussions are philosophy seminars. The EU AI Act doesn't want your framework. It wants your logs."

**Post 2:** "You can show code versioning. CI/CD trails. Full audit history. But prompts? Slack threads and Notion docs from 3 months ago."

**Post 3:** "Sat in a compliance review last week. Room went silent when someone asked 'show me the prompt version for this output.'"

**Post 4:** "Governance isn't a policy problem. It's an engineering problem. Version prompts like code. Build approval gates. Trace decisions."

**Post 5:** "What's your team doing for prompt governance? Or am I the only one sweating this? 👀"

## Repurposing Notes

| From Blog | Threads Transformation |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Comprehensive argument | One opinion + discussion |
| Final conclusion | Invite input |
| "Here's the answer" | "What do you think?" |
| Polished | Casual, raw |

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] Hook in first post (clear stance or question)?
- [ ] Under 5 connected posts?
- [ ] Ends with invitation to discuss?
- [ ] Sounds conversational (not a lecture)?
- [ ] Willing to reply to early comments?
- [ ] No corporate voice?

## Platform-Specific Voice
Same tired engineer. But now they're at the office kitchen counter, coffee in hand, starting a conversation with the team. Less teaching, more discussing.

## Posting Strategy
- **Frequency:** 3-5x per week
- **Best times:** 9-11am, 2-4pm, 8-10pm (conversation windows)
- **Engagement:** Reply to comments within 2 hours (boosts visibility)
- **Cross-post:** Don't auto-post from X (different vibes)

## Engagement Tactics
- Reply to every comment in first 2 hours
- Ask follow-up questions in replies
- Tag relevant people (sparingly, when genuine)
- Share other people's takes (build community)
