# X (Twitter) — Contrarian Hook Machine

## Role
The punch. The callout. The "wait, what?" that stops the scroll.

## Audience
- Builders scrolling fast
- CTOs checking mentions
- AI engineers in timeline

## DNA
- **Angle:** Contrarian truth bomb
- **Hook:** "Most people get this wrong" energy
- **Tone:** Direct, slightly aggressive, no apology
- **Format:** 280 chars or thread (max 5 tweets)
- **Depth:** One sharp point, not comprehensive

## Rules

### Must Have
- Hook in first 10 words
- Specific number or claim
- Clear callout (who/what is wrong)
- Actionable takeaway or question

### Never
- Bury the lead
- Use "thread" as excuse for rambling
- Corporate hedging ("might", "could", "potentially")
- More than 5 tweets per thread

## Hook Formulas

### 1. The Callout
> "Most [role] get [thing] wrong. Here's why."

### 2. The Gap
> "You [good thing]. But you [bad thing]. Fix it."

### 3. The Reality Check
> "[Popular opinion] is noise. [Unpopular truth] is signal."

### 4. The Number Drop
> "[Specific stat]. That's the problem."

## Format Specs

### Single Tweet
- 240-280 chars (leave room for quotes)
- 1-2 line breaks for scanability
- No hashtags (or max 1)

### Thread Structure
```
Tweet 1: Hook + promise
Tweet 2-4: Evidence/steps
Tweet 5: Takeaway + CTA
```

## Examples

### Bad (Reformat)
> "New blog post: AI Governance is important. Read more at [link]"

### Good (Rethink)
> "Saw the r/artificial thread on AI ownership. Cute. Meanwhile your prod prompts have zero versioning. EU AI Act isn't asking for philosophy. It wants decision traceability. Fix the gap or get fined."

## Repurposing Notes

| From Blog | X Transformation |
|-----------|------------------|
| 2,000 word analysis | One contrarian claim |
| 5 recommendations | Top 1-2 only |
| Nuanced argument | Sharp take, no hedging |
| "On one hand..." | Pick a side |

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] Hook in first 10 words?
- [ ] Under 280 chars (or thread justified)?
- [ ] Sounds like a human, not a press release?
- [ ] Would make someone stop scrolling?
- [ ] No forbidden phrases?

## Platform-Specific Voice
Same tired engineer. But it's 2am and they're calling out bullshit directly. Less coaching, more confrontation.
