# LinkedIn — Professional Narrative, Raw

## Role
The story. The "here's what actually happened" that builds authority without bragging.

## Audience
- EU CTOs/VPs at AI startups
- Engineering leaders facing compliance
- Peers who've been in the trenches

## DNA
- **Angle:** Personal narrative + professional lesson
- **Hook:** "I sat in a meeting..." or "Room full of smart people..."
- **Tone:** Professional but raw, tired engineer energy
- **Format:** 1,300-2,000 chars, short paragraphs
- **Depth:** Story + 3 actionable takeaways

## Rules

### Must Have
- Personal hook (I/we/you, not "companies")
- Specific moment or realization
- 3 concrete takeaways (numbered)
- Direct closing line

### Never
- Corporate humble-brag ("blessed", "honored")
- Vague inspiration ("the future is bright")
- More than 5 hashtags
- Wall of text (use line breaks)

## Hook Formulas

### 1. The Meeting
> "I sat in [situation]. Room full of [people]. Then [moment of truth]."

### 2. The Silence
> "We could show [good thing]. Then someone asked [hard question]. Silence."

### 3. The Realization
> "Spent [time] on [thing]. Turns out I was solving the wrong problem."

### 4. The Gap
> "You [do good thing]. But you [skip critical thing]. Here's the risk."

## Format Specs

- **Length:** 1,300-2,000 chars (sweet spot for engagement)
- **Paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences each, heavy line breaks
- **Numbers:** Use specific counts, not "many" or "several"
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 max, relevant only (#AI, #Engineering, #Governance)

## Structure

```
Hook (personal moment)
↓
Context (what was happening)
↓
Tension (the problem revealed)
↓
Insight (what I learned)
↓
Takeaways (3 numbered actions)
↓
Closing (direct callout)
```

## Examples

### Bad (Reformat)
> "Excited to share my latest article on AI governance! This comprehensive piece covers key considerations for modern enterprises. #AI #Leadership"

### Good (Rethink)
> "I sat in a compliance review yesterday. Room full of smart people. Panic setting in. We could show every line of code changed. Git history was clean. Then someone asked: 'Show me the prompt version that generated this output.' Silence. We had Slack threads. Notion docs from three months ago. Nothing structured. The EU AI Act isn't asking for philosophy. It wants audit trails. Here's what I told the team: 1. Map where prompts touch data. 2. Version them like code. 3. Build approval gates. This isn't about controlling AI. It's about controlling liability. Do the work."

## Repurposing Notes

| From Blog | LinkedIn Transformation |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Abstract argument | Personal story |
| General advice | 3 specific actions |
| "Organizations should" | "I told my team" |
| Comprehensive list | Top 3 only |

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] Personal hook (I/we/you)?
- [ ] 1,300+ chars?
- [ ] Short paragraphs with line breaks?
- [ ] 3 numbered takeaways?
- [ ] Direct closing line?
- [ ] No corporate humble-brag language?
- [ ] Sounds like a tired engineer, not a LinkedIn influencer?

## Platform-Specific Voice
Same tired engineer. But now they're in a boardroom, sleeves rolled up, telling the truth after a long day. Professional enough for the room, raw enough to be believed.

## Posting Strategy
- **Best days:** Tue-Thu
- **Best times:** 8-9am CET, 12-1pm CET, 5-6pm CET
- **Frequency:** 3-5x per week max (quality > quantity)
