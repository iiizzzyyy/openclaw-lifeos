# Newsletter — Deep, Personal, 1,500 Words

## Role
The behind-the-scenes. The "here's what I'm actually thinking" long-form.

## Audience
- Subscribers who opted in (high intent)
- People who want your full take
- Readers who save and re-read

## DNA
- **Angle:** Personal narrative + deep insight
- **Hook:** "I've been thinking about..." or "Something I can't shake..."
- **Tone:** Conversational, vulnerable, tired engineer energy
- **Format:** 1,500-2,500 words, email-delivered
- **Depth:** Full context, nuance allowed, but still direct

## Rules

### Must Have
- Personal hook (why this matters to you)
- One core thesis (not 5 different points)
- Actionable close (what to do Monday morning)
- Sign-off that sounds like a human

### Never
- Corporate newsletter voice ("In this issue...")
- Bury the lead (get to the point in first 200 words)
- More than one CTA
- Generic sign-off ("Best regards")

## Subject Line Formulas

### 1. The Question
> "Why does [thing] keep failing?"

### 2. The Confession
> "I was wrong about [thing]"

### 3. The Number
> "[X] things I learned about [topic]"

### 4. The Contrarian
> "Stop [common advice]. Do this instead."

### 5. The Personal
> "Something I can't shake"

## Structure

```
Subject Line (curiosity + relevance)
↓
Preview Text (complete the promise)
↓
Personal Hook (why I'm writing this)
↓
Context (what's happening, why it matters)
↓
Core Thesis (one main argument)
↓
Evidence/Examples (2-3 supporting points)
↓
Actionable Close (what to do)
↓
Human Sign-off
```

## Example (AI Governance Topic)

**Subject:** "The gap in your AI infrastructure"

**Preview:** "It's not the models. It's the prompts."

**Opening:**
> "I spent 6 hours yesterday debugging a model drift issue. Turns out someone changed a system prompt without logging it. Again. This isn't a one-off. It's a pattern I'm seeing across teams shipping AI products. And it's about to become a compliance nightmare when the EU AI Act hits..."

**Core Thesis:**
> "You have code versioning. You don't have prompt governance. That gap is where lawsuits live."

**Actionable Close:**
> "Three things to do this week:
> 1. Map where prompts touch user data (not where you think — where they actually do)
> 2. Start versioning them like code commits
> 3. Add approval gates for prod prompt changes
> 
> That's it. Start there."

**Sign-off:**
> "Build the rails first. Then drive the car.
> — Izzy"

| Comprehensive | Deep on one thesis |

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] Subject line creates curiosity (not clickbait)?
- [ ] Preview text completes the promise?
- [ ] Personal hook in first 100 words?
- [ ] One core thesis (not 5 different points)?
- [ ] 1,500-2,500 words (depth without rambling)?
- [ ] Actionable close (what to do Monday)?
- [ ] Human sign-off (not "Best regards")?
- [ ] One CTA max (reply, click, or forward)?

## Platform-Specific Voice
Same tired engineer. But now they're writing to you directly at 11pm, coffee cold, telling you the thing they've been thinking about all day. More vulnerable, more personal, same directness.

## Posting Strategy
- **Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly (consistency > frequency)
- **Best day:** Tuesday or Wednesday morning (high open rates)
- **Best time:** 6-8am local time of audience
- **Reply to replies:** Yes, personally (builds relationship)

## Tools
- ConvertKit, Beehiiv, Substack (hosting)
- Plain text emails (feel more personal than HTML)
- No images in body (or minimal — this is text-first)

## Metrics to Track
- Open rate (target: 40%+)
- Reply rate (target: 2%+)
- Forward rate (target: 1%+)
- Unsubscribe rate (target: <0.5% per send)
